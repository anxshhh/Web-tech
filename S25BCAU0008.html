The Double-Slit Experiment

Exploring the wave-particle duality of light and matter in quantum mechanics

Introduction

The double-slit experiment is one of the most remarkable demonstrations in physics. First performed by Thomas Young in 1801 with light, it revealed that waves can interfere, creating bright and dark fringes. Later, with electrons and other particles, it showed the mysterious dual nature of matter in quantum mechanics.

Key Equations

Path Difference:
ΔL = d · sinθ
Constructive Interference:
d · sinθ = mλ    (m = 0, ±1, ±2 …)
Destructive Interference:
d · sinθ = (m + ½)λ
Wave Function:
ψ(x,t) = A · ei(kx − ωt)
